Abstract

We introduce VCDF-X, he first multimodal and explainable video conferencing deepfake dataset and benchmark, comprising over 10,000 videos synthesized by seven state-of-the-art generation pipelines spanning portrait animation (LiveAvatar, OVI, SadTalker), face swap (FaceFusion, DeepLiveCam, VisoMaster), and identity-driven generation (Dream-ID), with virtual background augmentation to faithfully emulate real-world conferencing conditions.

The defining contribution of VCDF-X ois its explainability-first annotation framework: every sample is paired with pixel-level manipulation masks, natural language forgery descriptions, facial action units (FAUs), eye gaze trajectories, depth maps, face mesh and contour geometry, and remote photoplethysmography (rPPG) signals - the richest per-sample annotation stack in the deepfake detection literature. This multimodal grounding enables not only stronger detection, but transparent, human-interpretable reasoning about forgery evidence.

We scope VCDF-X to the visual modality, reflecting how real-world conferencing deepfake tools operate. Building on these modalities, we propose a novel explainable multimodal detector that fuses physiological, geometric, appearance, and semantic cues, and present a comprehensive benchmark evaluating state-of-the-art detectors under in-distribution, cross-method, and cross-background generalization settings.
